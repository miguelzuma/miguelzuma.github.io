Software

Over the years I have developed several software packages for cosmology and gravitational physics:

  • GLoW: Gravitational Lensing of Waves a flexible code for gravitatonal lensing in the wave-optics regime (with H. Villarrubia-Rojo, S. Savastano, L. Choi, S. Goyal, L. Dai and G. Tambalo)
  • Hi_CLASS: Horndeski in the Cosmic Linear Anisotropy Solving System an Einstein-Boltzmann solver for dark-energy and modified gravity (with E. Bellini, P. Ferreira, J. Lesgourgues and I. Sawicki)
  • Quintessence in CLASS, already integrated in the public version
  • A modified version of Latticeeasy to solve the non-linear equations describing disformally coupled theories (with Y. Murakami, D. Bettoni)
  • Other codes for LTB models (1201.2790), disformally coupled DE (1210.8016) and disformal quintessence (1004.2684) in CMBEasy

You can also visit my Github page to see what I have made public or email me if you are interested in codes not released yet.

Some codes that I like:

Boltzmann Codes:

My codes of choice to obtain predictions for models representing fundamental physics are CLASS and CMBEasy (the latter one becoming less useful, as it is not maintained). Although I use it less, I find the CAMB online tool very useful.

Tensor Calculus:

There are two excelent packages for Mathematica that I strongly recommend: xAct is able to handle symbolic computations within differential geometry in a very general way, but it is difficult to learn. RGTC is very easy to learn, but limited to tensor computations in a given set of coordinates.
